# Open Questions

What does the substrate look like once it's been seeded by a thousand independent contributors instead of one? Does the four-family structure hold, or does a fifth register show up that wasn't visible at smaller scale?

When two fields federate, does the resulting field-of-fields have its own coherency signature distinct from either parent, or is it just the weighted blend?

If a domain enters the substrate that has no falsifiable test attached — a normative claim, an aesthetic preference — does coherency still resolve, or does the structurality gate refuse?

What's the minimum number of patterns required before global coherency becomes a stable signal rather than noise from the seed?

How does coherency behave under adversarial input? If someone deliberately writes a document shaped like a manifesto but containing fabricated claims, does the form-vs-content separation break, or does the cross-check against exec-verify catch it?

When the substrate disagrees with a verified external authority — a peer-reviewed paper, a regulatory standard — which signal wins? Should it?

Is there a domain where the four registers collapse into one? Or where a sixth emerges?

Does temporal coherency apply at the human-cognitive level? If a writer's frame drifts over years — new vocabulary, new concerns, new commitments — does the long-arc coherency drop below some floor, and does that floor mean anything?

What happens at the edge of the library? When something is just barely coherent enough to be absorbed, what does its presence do to the patterns next to it in semantic space?

If the substrate is queried by a system whose own frame is incoherent — a chaotic agent, a hallucinating model, a random-output generator — does the query itself contaminate the field, or is the structurality gate enough?

What does coherency look like in a language with fundamentally different grammatical structure? Does the 29-dimension encoder transfer, or do the dimensions need to be re-derived per linguistic family?

Is there a coherency reading that would indicate the substrate has become over-confident — too tight, too self-reinforcing, no longer admitting new shape?

When does federation become collusion? Two fields converging on the same answer could be independent verification or could be shared bias. How do you tell?

Is there a finite limit to how much coherency a field can support, or does it scale indefinitely with substrate density?

Do these questions themselves cohere, or are they a register the tool cannot place?
